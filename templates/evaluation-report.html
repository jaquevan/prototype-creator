How Usability Scores Are Calculated

INF (Inference) — fast, structural analysis from Playwright journey evidence. TA (Think-Aloud) — slow, diagnostic persona narration.

7 dimensions scored 0-3: Workflow Continuity, Cross-Persona Handoffs, Scalability, System Status, Technical Abstraction, Mental Model Fidelity, Accessibility. Composite = 21.

Patience model: starts 100%, confusion costs 5-15%, dead ends cost 10-30%, successes recover 5-10%. At 0% the persona abandons.

This is an eval report

It checks whether a prototype meets the acceptance criteria from its Jira ticket. An automated evaluator ran Playwright walkthroughs against the live prototype, navigating pages, clicking through flows, and checking what it found against what the ticket requires.

The verdict

The banner at the top of the report tells you the overall result. There are three possible outcomes:

✓
PASS
All acceptance criteria from the Jira ticket were met. You can approve or skim the evidence.
✗
NEEDS FIXES
Some criteria failed. The report lists which ones and shows the evidence for each.
!
NEEDS REVIEW
Some criteria couldn't be verified automatically. They need your judgment.
1 / 3

Your role

Most of the report is automated. But items marked FLAGGED are the ones that need you.

The automated check couldn't make the call on these — maybe it requires comparing against an external system, or the criterion is subjective. Click any flagged item to see the evidence, then make the final call: pass or fail.

If the verdict is PASS and nothing is flagged, there is nothing for you to do — the report is informational.

2 / 3

Finding your way

The report is split into two panels:

Left panel Your results — the verdict, acceptance criteria with pass/fail/flagged verdicts, and usability scores.
Right panel The evidence — persona walkthrough screenshots, findings, and design consistency checks. Click items on the left to populate this.

Expand Full Evidence & Details on the left for the complete picture — iteration history, usability dimensions, and all the supporting data.
