How Usability Scores Are Calculated
Two scoring layers:

INF (Inference) — fast, structural analysis. The evaluator applies persona knowledge constraints to the Playwright journey evidence and scores each dimension based on observable UI patterns. Good for CI and regression testing.

TA (Think-Aloud) — slow, diagnostic analysis. The evaluator role-plays as a persona navigating the prototype step by step, producing a first-person internal monologue. Reveals false confidence, wrong mental models, guess-and-pray patterns, and cumulative frustration that inference misses.

Composite score: average across evaluated personas per dimension. Overall is sum of composites out of 21.

Confidence levels: High = structural blocker directly observed. Medium = friction consistent with persona profile. Low = logical inference from constraints.

Patience model: each persona starts at 100%. Confusion costs 5-15%, dead ends cost 10-30%, successful sub-tasks recover 5-10%. At 0% the persona abandons.
