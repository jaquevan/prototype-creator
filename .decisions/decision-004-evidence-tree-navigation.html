Evidence Tree Navigation

Interaction

Now that we know the tree lives in a left sidebar, how does it actually work? When an AC filter is active, some steps have evidence (orange) and some don't. The question is: how do non-evidence steps behave, how is evidence density communicated, and what visual language indicates "this step matters for the selected AC."

Non-evidence steps dim to 40% opacity but remain visible and clickable. Evidence steps show a solid orange dot. A count badge on each persona header shows how many evidence steps exist for the active filter. The selected step gets a subtle background highlight. I recommend this because it preserves full walkthrough context (you can still see the journey) while making evidence steps visually pop.

Works well when

  • Designer wants context — seeing the full journey, not just evidence
  • Quick scan of evidence density per persona (count badge)
  • Non-evidence steps still accessible for context

Watch out for

  • Dimmed items still take vertical space
  • With 10+ steps, the tree can get long even when only 3 matter

When an AC filter is active, non-evidence steps are hidden entirely — only evidence steps show. A "+ N other steps" link at the bottom of each persona allows expanding to see the full journey if needed. The tree is radically shorter — you only see what matters. Step numbers are preserved (2, 3, 6 — not renumbered) so you know where in the journey each piece of evidence lives.

Works well when

  • Evidence is sparse (3 of 10 steps) — no clutter
  • Quick comparison across personas — both fit on screen
  • Step numbers show gaps, revealing journey position

Watch out for

  • Loses walkthrough context — can't see what happened between evidence
  • Expanding "+ N other steps" adds complexity
  • Jump in step numbers may confuse (2, 3, 6 — where's 4-5?)

Green=success · Red=fail · Orange=AC evidence
Every step always shows with a colored dot indicating its state: green (step succeeded), red (step failed/confusion), orange (evidence for active AC filter). All steps remain full opacity. The tree doubles as a minimap showing both the walkthrough outcome AND evidence location. More information-dense but more colors to parse.

Works well when

  • Designer wants step-level success/failure alongside evidence
  • Full walkthrough context always visible
  • Orange dots "pop" against green/red background

Watch out for

  • Three dot colors + active state = complex visual language
  • Green/red verdicts may not exist at step level (only journey level)
  • When no AC filter is active, what color are the dots?

Uses a single color (orange) with two states: filled circle = evidence, unfilled ring = no evidence. All steps stay visible at full opacity. The contrast between solid fill and ring outline is subtle but clear. No dimming, no hiding, no extra colors. The simplest visual vocabulary of all four options.

Works well when

  • Minimal color language — one hue, two states
  • Full walkthrough always visible, no dimming
  • Accessible — doesn't rely on opacity differences

Watch out for

  • Ring vs filled is subtle at small sizes
  • Non-evidence steps are equally prominent (no visual priority)
  • Less "pop" for evidence steps compared to dim+dot
