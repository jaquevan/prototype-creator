Decision 1 of 1: Visual Direction — SCOUT Dashboard visual

Choose the retro-themed visual identity for the SCOUT eval dashboard. The mascot is an animated seahorse — the "scout" that explores prototypes. Each option applies to the dashboard, stats cards, report table, and the Leaderboard of Pain page.

Deep navy and cream parchment tones evoke a captain's log. Serif headlines via Playfair Display pair with monospace data. The seahorse is rendered in a vintage engraving style. Stats appear as brass instrument gauges.

Pros

  • Rich, distinguished aesthetic
  • Excellent data readability with monospace
  • Maritime theme fits "scout" metaphor naturally

Cons

  • Can feel heavy or formal
  • Serif fonts less common in dev tools
  • Limited animation opportunities

The pixel/retro game aesthetic makes evaluation feel playful rather than clinical. Each prototype is a "level" the seahorse explores. The dark theme with neon accents is distinctive and immediately recognizable — no one will confuse this with any other tool.

Pros

  • Immediately distinctive and memorable
  • Dark theme reduces eye strain
  • Rich animation potential (sprites, transitions)
  • Game metaphor makes evals fun

Cons

  • Pixel fonts harder to read at small sizes
  • May feel unserious to some stakeholders
  • Dark theme requires careful contrast work

Pros

Cons

  • Could feel too "fun" for serious issues
  • Bubble shapes waste some screen real estate

Leaderboard of Pain Fit Adequate — "most wanted" poster style Excellent — high score table, boss battles, skull sprites for worst offenders Good — mission failure board, countdown style Good — "dishonorable discharge" board, revoked badges
How to decide: Review each preview above and imagine your daily workflow — opening the SCOUT dashboard to check which prototypes passed, which failed, and clicking through to the Leaderboard of Pain. Which visual world do you want to spend time in? The recommended option (B: Pixel Explorer) prioritizes distinctiveness and delight, but if your team values gravitas (A) or warmth (D), those are strong choices too.
