Review Actions & Output

Interaction

At the bottom of the review panel, what action buttons are available? The designer has finished reviewing flagged items, typed notes, and overridden verdicts. Now they need to DO something with that work. The question is what actions are offered and how they behave.

A progress checklist at the top showing which items are reviewed. Below: two stacked buttons. "Download Updated CSV" generates a CSV with the designer's notes and verdict overrides merged in. "Request Re-iteration" copies a formatted prompt to clipboard that the designer can paste into a new eval-iterate run. I recommend this because it's the simplest path — download is instant, re-iteration is explicit.

Works well when

  • Clear separation — download is one action, re-run is another
  • Progress checklist gives overview of what's done
  • No ambiguity about what each button does

Watch out for

  • "Request Re-iteration" copies to clipboard — not a direct trigger
  • Two buttons may look like a sequence when they're independent

The action bar changes based on review state. Mid-review: only "Save progress" and "Download CSV (partial)". Once all items are reviewed: the full action set appears — "Download Final CSV", "Request New Iteration" (prominent green), and "Close Panel". Creates a sense of completion and gates the re-iteration behind finishing all reviews.

Works well when

  • Prevents accidental re-run before review is complete
  • Clear progression: review → complete → act
  • Auto-save means no lost work

Watch out for

  • Gating actions may frustrate if user wants to re-run after 1 review
  • More complex state management

One large primary button that does both: downloads the updated CSV AND copies the re-iteration prompt. The default "happy path" is one click. Below it, smaller buttons for "just download" or "just re-run" if the designer wants only one action. Optimized for the common case where you want both.

Works well when

  • One-click happy path — most designers want both
  • Still offers individual actions as escape hatches
  • Feels decisive — "I'm done, let's go"

Watch out for

  • Combining two actions in one button can be confusing
  • What if they don't want a re-run?

Every individual save triggers a toast confirmation ("Saved. CSV will include this override."). The action buttons are always visible at the bottom of the panel regardless of review progress — no gating. Download is blue (neutral), re-run is red-tinted (destructive, triggers a new eval cycle). Each action is independent and always available.

Works well when

  • Designer wants to re-run after reviewing just one item
  • No forced workflow — maximum flexibility
  • Toast feedback confirms each save immediately

Watch out for

  • Red "Re-run" button might trigger accidental re-runs
  • No completion state — panel never says "you're done"
