AC Filter Interaction

Interaction

The filter bar at the top of the evidence viewer lets the designer select which AC to focus on. When they select one, the tree updates (orange dots appear on evidence steps, others dim). The question is: what's the selection model? Chips, dropdown, or something else? And what happens on selection — instant highlight, animated transition, auto-navigation?

Horizontal chip row — one chip per AC, plus an "All" chip that clears the filter. Only one AC can be active at a time (radio behavior). On selection: tree highlights instantly, and the viewer auto-navigates to the first evidence step. The designer lands directly on relevant content. I recommend this because it matches the ticket spec ("pre-filled and pre-filtered to the relevant AC and step") and removes a click from the flow.

Works well when

  • Pre-filling from the AC table works naturally (click "View Evidence" → chip is already active)
  • Auto-navigate means zero additional clicks to see evidence
  • Chip row is always visible — easy to switch between ACs

Watch out for

  • Auto-navigation can feel jarring if the user was mid-exploration
  • 8+ ACs will overflow the chip row (needs horizontal scroll)

A dropdown select showing the full AC text + verdict badge. Saves horizontal space (only shows active selection). On selection: tree highlights update but the current view stays put — no auto-navigation. The designer keeps control of where they are and navigates to evidence when ready. Shows the full AC text rather than just "AC-1".

Works well when

  • Many ACs (8+) — dropdown scales better than chips
  • Full AC text visible in dropdown helps recognition
  • No jarring auto-navigation — designer stays in control

Watch out for

  • Extra click to open dropdown before selecting
  • Dropdown hides all other ACs when closed
  • Pre-fill from AC table is less elegant (opens dropdown?)

Same chip row as A, but each chip is colored by its verdict — green for PASS, red for FAIL, amber for FLAGGED. The designer can see at a glance which ACs have problems and click directly to those. Auto-navigates on selection like Option A. Adds verdict information to the filter itself.

Multi-select chips — the designer can activate multiple ACs simultaneously, and the tree shows the union of all evidence steps. Steps that match multiple selected ACs get a special indicator. Useful for exploring relationships between criteria, but more complex to parse and doesn't auto-navigate (ambiguous target).

Works well when

  • Designer wants to see overlap between ACs (which steps serve multiple criteria)
  • Exploring broadly before diving into one specific AC
  • Advanced usage — power-user flexibility

Watch out for

  • Can't auto-navigate (no single target step)
  • Multi-select is harder to pre-fill from AC table
  • More complex mental model — most users only need one AC at a time
